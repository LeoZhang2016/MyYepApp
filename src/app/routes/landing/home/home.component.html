What Is Buying off The Plan?

No doubt you’ve heard the term in the past, usually when in conversation about proprietary investments or home ownership options. To put it simply, when you purchase off plan property, you’re investing in an unfinished product, wherein the construction is yet to begin or be completed.

Typically, you’re purchasing a concept home, based on the designs given by the architect and contractors, oftentimes coming in this early lends itself to more potent benefits for the overall design and final product.

In many instances, this allows for a more affordable upfront payment for the home, as well as allowing some degree of wiggle room when it comes to financial strains that are more commonplace with ready-built purchases.

Is Off The Plan Buying A Wiser Investment?

Depending on your circumstance, it can be a very good option to buy off plan property, whether it be for your portfolio or your very first home. For those who are looking at budgetary constraints and are not in the finest financial position, this is an excellent method of getting into the market without having to leverage your entire life on it.

Another wise association with off the plan buying comes from the savings that are often found in the long-term for what you end up receiving. The locations for many prospective sites are in up-and-coming locales, close to transport, conveniently located, and unabashedly modern, well below what you’d normally pay.

The trade-off is of course that it will take some time for your investment to come to fruition, so a forward-thinking plan is necessary as it will often be a certain degree of time for the construction to be completed.

Common Queries Surrounding
Off The Plan Buying

We get asked a lot of questions surrounding the process and financials of off plan houses and apartments.

Will I Have To Pay For
The Entire Bill At Once?

In short, no. You are required to pay a deposit when the contracts are exchanged which is typical for any off the plan purchase. The good news is, you are not required to make payments on the remainder until construction is finished. So, you can start saving in your own way right up until the day the builders are finished on your brand-new home.

Can I Inspect Before
Paying?

Unfortunately, as off the plan means that construction is not yet underway or completed, it is impossible to physically inspect the prospective purchase. However, we are determined to give you every piece of information possible, and through the developers, we are able to showcase concept artworks and everything that we know about the property.
There are a number of stringent laws surrounding off the plan purchases that ensure that buyers have all the information they could possibly need to make their decision in good faith which we abide by completely.

How Long Will It Be
Before I Can Move In?

Each construction is a little different, so the variable times you will have to wait will depend on the location, the scale of construction, as well as how far the developers are in the process of drafting and building. When it comes to buying off the plan property, you can expect to wait a while for your investment to flourish, but it will be well worth it when it does.
